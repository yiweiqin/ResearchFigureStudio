# Journal Quality Checklist

## Summary

Use this checklist before returning a figure intended for a paper, thesis, or
serious research presentation.

## Paper Grounding

- The figure's modules and labels come from the user's manuscript.
- Any inferred simplification is marked as such in the working notes.
- The figure supports a specific paper claim.
- Decorative imagery does not imply mechanisms that the paper does not claim.

## Framework Figures

- Visual blocks are stylistically consistent.
- Visual blocks fill their slot: useful content is normally 88%-95% of the
  canvas and at least 80%, with no large blank border.
- Flow direction is obvious within three seconds.
- Arrows have unambiguous meaning.
- Groups and phases are visible without clutter.
- Critical labels, formulas, arrows, and panel marks are editable.
- The final figure still works when printed in grayscale or viewed small.

## Data Figures

- Values match the source data.
- Axis labels include units or metric definitions where needed.
- Legends do not cover data.
- Labels and tick marks do not overlap.
- Uncertainty and statistical tests are documented when shown.
- Color choices are colorblind-safe and consistent across related panels.

## Publication Export

- `editable_composition.pptx` is present when the figure was intended to be
  manually editable.
- Critical labels, arrows, formulas, legends, and panel IDs remain editable in
  PPT, not baked into a flattened bitmap.
- Vector content may be exported as PDF/SVG when the venue or downstream tool
  requires it.
- Image-heavy figures are exported at 600 DPI or better.
- Fonts are embedded or converted safely for submission.
- Line weights and font sizes survive single-column/double-column scaling.
- File names clearly distinguish editable working files from final exports.

## Final Response

When delivering, state:

- what source text/data the figure was based on
- which figure route was used
- which files are editable and which are submission exports
- any assumptions or scientific ambiguities that need author review
